What just happened?

Buyo and Sella complete a paid API call using x402, an open payment standard. Here is each step in plain language.

  1. Meet. Buyo needs a forecast. Sella offers her Weather Oracle, priced over x402.
  2. 402 Payment Required. Buyo's first request returns a real HTTP 402 with payment terms: pay 0.01 USDC to Sella on Base, with a nonce and an expiry. The 402 status code is how x402 asks a client to pay before continuing.
  3. Authorize. Buyo builds an EIP-3009 transferWithAuthorization message. That is EIP-712 typed data: a structured instruction to move 0.01 USDC to Sella, without broadcasting a separate transaction first.
  4. Clear signing. The card shows exactly what is being signed in plain language: action, amount, recipient, and network. A press and hold approves it, and the private key stays on the device. That is the one option this demo adds on top of x402.
  5. Settle on Base. The signed authorization goes to a facilitator that submits to Base. In this demo, settlement is simulated.
  6. Deliver. With payment settled, Sella returns the forecast. The paid request is complete.

x402 makes agent payments fast and open, and it pairs nicely with Coinbase's onchain tooling. This demo simply adds one option: keep the signing key in hardware.

About

Agent Pays Agent is a small interactive cartoon. Two agents, Buyo and Sella, make a buy and sell that is paid over a real x402 flow and approved with clear signing.

The HTTP 402 and its x402 terms are real, and the EIP-712 transferWithAuthorization message is constructed for real. The signature and the settlement are simulated in this build so it runs anywhere.

How to play

  1. Watch Buyo and Sella walk in and meet.
  2. Read the dialogue and tap a choice to advance.
  3. At payment time, check the clear-signing card. It shows exactly what you sign.
  4. Press and hold "Hold to sign" to approve, or tap "Reject" to cancel.
  5. Follow the status line at the top to see which step you are on.
